Midwestern Chili Recipe

A savory classic dish with a twist!

Chili goes way back to the 1800s when cowboys were out working on the trail and tossing together whatever they had. From day one it was basically an experiment people slapped it on tamales, dumped it over cornbread, even used it for early Frito pies. So honestly, chili never had one “correct” version. That's why when people act like the way we make it in the Midwest is weird, I just laugh. This dish was freestyled since the beginning. We're not doing anything crazy. We're just carrying on the tradition of “cook what you've got and make it taste good.”

Ingredients

  • 1 lb lean ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • can (15 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder "optional, but recommended for depth"
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground allspice
  • 1/2 tsp ground cloves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Brown the ground beef and onion in a large pot, then drain excess fat.
  2. Stir in garlic, chili powder, Worcestershire sauce, and all spices, cooking for about a minute.
  3. Add tomato sauce and beef broth, then simmer uncovered for at least an hour.
  4. Serve over spaghetti with desired toppings. A "five-way" includes chili, spaghetti, cheese, onions, and beans.

Optional Add-ons!

  • Shredded cheddar cheese
  • Kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • Chopped onions
  • Oyster crackers
  • Spaghett
  • Baked Potato
  • French Fries
  • Whatever your little heart desires!
