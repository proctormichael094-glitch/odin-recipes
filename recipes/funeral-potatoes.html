Funeral Potato Recipe

A cheesy comfort food for the soul!

Funeral potatoes are a Midwestern classic dish. It's known for it's creamy and cheesy insides mixed with diced potatoes, while also having a crunchy top. It originally was created in the late 19th century as an easy way to fill your belly. But over the decades it has become a comfort staple for people who have fallen on hard times. You can find it at most funeral after service luncheons. It also makes for a delicious warm meal paired with your choice of protein during the winter.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups sour cream
  • 10.5 ounce can cream of chicken soup (or homemade)
  • 10 Tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried minced onion
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 cups corn flakes cereal
